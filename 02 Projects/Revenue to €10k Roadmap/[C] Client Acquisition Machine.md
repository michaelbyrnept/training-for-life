## 1. The Free Capability Program — Turn Downloads Into Clients

**The problem with most free programs:** They have high download rates and terrible completion rates. If someone doesn't complete it, they have no transformation, no trust, and no reason to upgrade.

**Your goal:** Maximise completions, not downloads.

### Before They Start

Add a Capability Score assessment at the very beginning. 5 simple tests:

1. How long can you hold a wall sit? (legs at 90 degrees)
2. How many push-ups can you do without stopping?
3. Balance test: stand on one leg, eyes closed, how long?
4. Grip squeeze (or estimate: can you open a tight jar easily?)
5. Can you get off the floor without using your hands?

Score them 0-100. Tell them: "Your Capability Score is 54/100. That puts you at the physical capability of the average 58-year-old."

**Why this works:** Loss aversion. Nobody wants to be told they're performing like someone 10-15 years older. It creates urgency without a single sales pitch. The score also creates a before/after moment when they rescore after the program.

### During the Program

- Send one message per day (push notification or email). Not "Don't forget to train!" but something specific: "Day 2 is about hip strength. Most people ignore this. Here's why it matters..."
- Make it feel like Michael is coaching them personally, not just sending content.
- Day 2 include one check-in question: "How sore are you after Day 1? (1-5)" — this creates engagement and gives you data.

### After They Complete It

Rescore them. Even a small improvement (say, 54 to 61) feels massive when it's framed right.

Send this message on Day 4 (one day after program ends):

> "You just completed the 3-Day Capability Program. Most people never finish what they start, so you're already in a different category. Your score moved from 54 to 61. Want to know what 90 days of focused training could do? I offer free 20-minute Capability Reviews — no sales pitch, just an honest look at where you could go. Book one here: [link]"

**Conversion path:** Assessment → emotional investment → program → visible result → natural next step.

**Expected conversion:** If you get 100 program completions per month, 20-30 will book consultations with this approach. Of those, 10-15 become clients.

## 2. Gym Floor Marketing — The GPS Approach

Most trainers either say nothing (missed opportunity) or pitch too hard (damages trust). The GPS approach avoids both.

**GPS = Give → Position → Suggest**

### Give

Offer something genuinely useful before you say anything about your services.

Examples:
- "Hey, I noticed your setup on the Romanian deadlift. One cue that might help — drive your hips back rather than down. Try it?"
- "Are you finding the squat rack is always taken at this time? The one upstairs is usually free until 7."
- "How long have you been working on your overhead press? I've been seeing you at this for a few weeks — it's coming along."

The goal is to be the trainer who notices people, not the trainer who approaches people looking for a sale.

### Position

After 1-3 positive interactions (same person, different days), drop in organically:

> "I've been building an app for the members here. It's completely free — it has all the programs I give to my PT clients. Might be useful if you're following a plan."

You're not pitching. You're sharing a tool. That's a completely different psychological register.

### Suggest

When they show any interest:

> "Scan this and it'll take you straight to the 3-Day Capability Program. It's what I give everyone who's starting to get serious about training. Takes about 20-30 minutes a day."

The QR code should link directly to the 3-Day Program, not the homepage.

### The Sequence in Practice

- Day 1: Give (helpful observation, no ask)
- Day 3-5: Give again, acknowledge them ("Back again — good")
- Day 7-10: Position (mention the app as a tool)
- Day 14+: Suggest (QR code to 3-Day Program)

Never rush this. The people who become your best clients are the ones who chose you, not the ones you convinced.

## 3. Group Classes — Before, During, After

Group classes are one of the highest-leverage opportunities you have. People are already doing something hard. They're already in the identity of "someone who trains." You just need to move them from "class participant" to "coaching client."

### Before Class

Post a 60-second clip on your story the morning of each class. Not "Join my class at 6pm!" but something like: "In tonight's class we're working on single-leg strength. Most people skip this. Here's why it matters for your knees long-term..."

This pre-educates. People arrive thinking of you as a coach, not just an instructor.

### During Class

Two things that work:

1. **The Name Rule:** Use people's names at least twice per class. This sounds obvious but most instructors don't do it consistently. It creates belonging.

2. **Public Progress Acknowledgement:** Once per class, call out someone's improvement publicly. "Sarah's been coming every Tuesday for two months, and her squat depth has completely transformed — Sarah, show them." This does three things: rewards loyalty, demonstrates that progress is visible, and makes others want their moment.

### After Class

The 3-minute window after class is the most valuable time you have. Most people linger. This is where real conversations happen.

Have a specific question ready each week. Not "How was that?" (bland) but: "Which bit challenged you most today?" or "Have you noticed any difference in how you feel day-to-day since you've been coming?"

These open the door to real coaching conversations naturally.

### Inside the App

If class participants are on the app, send them a relevant message after class: "If you were in Tuesday's class, here's the 5-minute cool-down I'd add tonight to help your recovery..."

This shows value beyond the class and positions the app as an extension of the coaching relationship.

### The Upgrade Trigger

If someone has attended 8+ classes, they're a warm lead. A natural conversation: "You've been showing up consistently — that's the hard part. Have you thought about what you'd want to work on beyond classes? I do a free 20-minute review for people who want a clearer direction."

## 4. Referral Systems — Dropbox Not MLM

Cash referral incentives feel transactional. They also attract people who referred for the money, not because they genuinely love your service. Here's a better model.

### The Guest Session Model

Every coaching client gets 2 guest passes per quarter. These let them bring a friend to a session with them.

**Why this is better than cash referrals:**
- The friend experiences the coaching directly, not just hears about it
- It's a gift, not a pitch
- The existing client becomes an advocate because they get to show off their trainer
- Conversion rate from experienced referrals is 5-10x higher than from word-of-mouth alone

### The Milestone Trigger

When a client hits a milestone (first month, 10kg lost, first unassisted pull-up), send them a personal message acknowledging it. Then:

> "You've made serious progress. I'd love to help someone you know get started the same way. I have one guest session slot open this month if you know anyone who'd benefit."

The psychology: they're in a peak positive emotional state. That's the moment to ask.

### The "Send This to a Friend" Piece of Content

Create one genuinely useful thing per month that's easy to forward. Not "Share my services" but something like: "Here's the exact 10-minute morning routine I give all my clients over 40 — feel free to pass it on."

When someone shares useful content, they're implicitly endorsing you.

## 5. Previous Client Reactivation

These are the highest-probability new clients you have. They already know you work. You don't need to build trust from scratch.

**Segment your previous clients into three buckets:**
- Left on good terms, life got busy
- Left because of money
- Left for unknown reasons

The approach is slightly different for each, but the opening is always personal, never generic.

### The "I Was Thinking of You" Message

Text or email, not both at once. Sent personally, not via a campaign tool.

> "Hey [Name], hope you're doing well. I was thinking about you — you were making great progress on [specific thing] when we last worked together. How's training been since?"

This gets responses. Not "Hi, here's my current offer." Just genuine follow-up.

Wait for the response. Then offer the consult naturally.

### Seasonal Reactivation Campaigns

**January (New Year):** "What's the one physical thing you want to feel different about in 2027? I'm taking on 3 clients in January who are serious about making it happen. Want to chat?"

**September (Back to Routine):** "Summer's over and people are getting back to structure. If you've been wanting to restart training, September is the best month to do it. I have a spot."

**Post-holiday (post-Christmas, post-summer):** "If energy's been low and motivation's not quite there — that's actually the best time to book a session. Not to punish yourself. Just to feel like yourself again."

### The 6-Month Check-In Sequence

Automate this via email:

- 30 days after last session: "Checking in — how's everything going?"
- 90 days: "I've added [new feature/program] since you were last here. Thought of you."
- 180 days: "Half a year since we trained together. Curious how you're feeling physically?"
- 12 months: "A year on. You made real progress when we worked together. What's changed since?"

These should feel personal. Use merge tags for their name and the specific thing they worked on.

## 8. Social Media — What to Post and Why

**Three content pillars:**

### Pillar 1: Education (40% of posts)

Teach one specific, useful thing per post. Not generic tips. Specific mechanics.

"Why your lower back hurts after deadlifts (and it's not what you think)"
"The 3-minute test that reveals how well your hips are working"
"Why walking is the most underrated strength exercise for people over 40"

**Hook formula:** "Most people believe [wrong thing]. Here's what's actually happening."

**Format:** Short video (60-90 seconds) or carousel. Not long captions. Make the content skimmable.

### Pillar 2: Proof (30% of posts)

Not before/after photos. Client journey moments.

"Mark came to me 6 months ago struggling to walk up stairs without his knees aching. Yesterday he completed a 5K. Here's what changed."

Frame results as: what they could do before → what triggered the change → what they can do now.

**Why this converts better than before/after:** People see themselves in the journey. Before/after feels unattainable. "What triggered the change" creates hope and a path.

### Pillar 3: Perspective (30% of posts)

Michael's philosophy on training for life, longevity, capability. Short, punchy, confident.

"Fitness isn't about punishment. It's about not being punished by your own body when you're 70."
"The goal isn't six-pack abs. It's being able to carry your grandkids."
"I don't care what you weigh. I care what you can do."

**These work because:** People share views they agree with. Every share is an endorsement.

### Posting Frequency

3-4 times per week minimum. Quality over quantity. One excellent educational video per week beats seven mediocre posts.

**Batch your content.** Block 2 hours every Monday. Film 3-4 clips. Schedule them. Done.

### Calls to Action

Rotate between three CTAs:
1. "Link in bio to grab the free 3-Day Capability Program"
2. "DM me 'SCORE' and I'll send you your free Capability Score"
3. "Book a free 20-minute Capability Review at [link]"

## 10. Lead Magnets — What Would People Actually Want

The best lead magnets are specific, measurable, and slightly confronting. They reveal something the person didn't know about themselves.

### Lead Magnet 1: The Capability Score (Highest Priority)

As described in Section 1. Build this as an interactive page on your website. Requires email to get results.

5 tests, 5 minutes, one score out of 100. Output: "Your score is X. Here's what it means and what would improve it."

This is the one to build first. Everything else should drive people here.

### Lead Magnet 2: Strength Age Calculator

"How old is your body, really?"

4 strength tests mapped to age norms. Tells someone: "Your chronological age is 42. Your strength age is 51."

The psychology: identity threat + specific hope. Nobody ignores this.

### Lead Magnet 3: The 7-Day Mobility Reset

A free 7-day email course. One 5-minute mobility routine per day with video. Targets people with tight hips, stiff backs, or desk-related aches.

This works because it solves a specific, painful, daily problem. Every person who finishes it trusts you more.

### Lead Magnet 4: The Longevity Checklist

"10 signs your body is aging faster than it should (and what to do about each one)"

Downloadable PDF. Drives email collection. Each "sign" has a simple correction that demonstrates your expertise.

## 11. Local Community — Highest ROI Partnerships

### GP and Physio Partnerships (Priority 1)

This is the highest-value channel you're likely not using.

GPs and physios regularly see patients with: chronic back pain, post-surgery recovery, age-related weakness, metabolic issues, anxiety/depression where exercise is recommended.

These patients need someone to send them to. You want to be that person.

**The approach:**
- Visit 3-5 local practices in person. Introduce yourself. Bring a one-page overview of what you do.
- Offer: "I specialise in training people who need to rebuild strength safely, often after they've finished physio. If you ever have a patient who needs this kind of support, I'd be happy to offer them a complimentary assessment."
- Leave cards and your QR code.

**Why this works:** GPs are trusted authorities. A recommendation from a GP carries more weight than any ad you could run. One referral from a GP practice is worth months of social media content.

**Expected ROI:** If one GP sends you 2 patients per month, and those patients pay €150/month, that's €300/month minimum from a single relationship. With 3 GP partnerships: €900/month in new revenue.

### Corporate Wellness (Priority 2)

One corporate contract can be worth €2,000-5,000 for a single workshop series, or an ongoing arrangement where employees get discounted coaching.

**The pitch:** HR departments are under pressure on employee wellbeing KPIs. Your offer: a monthly 45-minute workshop ("Train Your Body to Handle Your Job"), plus discounted coaching for staff.

**Target companies:** Medium-sized companies (50-200 staff) in Dublin. Tech companies, financial services, and insurance firms typically have wellness budgets.

**Opening line:** "I help office workers prevent the physical damage that desk jobs do over a career. I run a monthly workshop and offer coaching packages for employees. I'd love 20 minutes to show you what other companies have found useful."

### Running Clubs and Sports Groups

Runners are already exercisers. They understand the value of coaching. They're often injured.

Position yourself as "the strength coach for runners" not "a PT who also works with runners."

Offer to run a free 30-minute session at their next group run: "The 3 strength exercises every runner should be doing (and almost none do)."

This demonstrates expertise in front of 10-40 people in one session.

## 12. Premium Offers — What to Charge and How to Sell It

The psychology of premium: people pay high prices for guaranteed outcomes and white-glove experience. Price is rarely the objection when the outcome is specific and believable.

### €500 — 4-Week Capability Kickstart

**What it includes:**
- 3 x 1-on-1 sessions per week (12 total)
- Full capability assessment at start and end
- App access with a personalised training plan
- Weekly check-in message

**Who it's for:** Someone who wants to get serious quickly. Often a new client who wants a jump-start.

**How to sell it:** "In 4 weeks, you'll know exactly where you stand physically, you'll have a training habit locked in, and you'll have a plan you can follow independently. Most clients continue to coaching after this."

### €1,000 — 12-Week Transformation

**What it includes:**
- 2 x 1-on-1 sessions per week
- Weekly 30-min coaching call
- Personalised training + nutrition framework
- Direct WhatsApp access during the program
- Before and after Capability Score

**Who it's for:** Someone committed to a specific outcome. Works well for people with a milestone (holiday, event, milestone birthday).

**How to sell it:** "In 12 weeks, here's exactly what we'll build: [specific outcome]. I've done this with [X] clients. Here's what it looks like for someone like you."

### €5,000+ — VIP Annual Coaching Membership

**What it includes:**
- Monthly 1-on-1 session
- Unlimited app access
- Quarterly Capability Review
- Priority scheduling (your schedule around theirs, within reason)
- Direct line to Michael for questions, adjustments, check-ins
- Annual training roadmap

**Who it's for:** Busy professionals who want the best result with the least friction. They're paying for access and personalisation, not just sessions.

**How to sell it:** Don't list the features. Sell the identity: "You'd have a coach who knows your body, your schedule, and your goals. Everything is handled. You just show up."

**Pricing psychology:** At €5,000/year, this is €417/month. Frame it monthly: "Less than the cost of a gym membership with a personal trainer, but with someone who actually knows your history."
