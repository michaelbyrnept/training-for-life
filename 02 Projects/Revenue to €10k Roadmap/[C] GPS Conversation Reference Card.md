# GPS Gym Floor Reference Card

**Give. Position. Suggest.**

Use this on the gym floor. Memorise the structure. Adapt the words naturally.

---

## The Rule

Never pitch on the first contact. Never. The goal of every first interaction is to be the trainer who *noticed* them, not the trainer who *approached* them.

**Timeline:**
- Day 1: Give (no mention of you or your services)
- Day 3-5: Give again
- Day 7-10: Position (mention the app)
- Day 14+: Suggest (QR code)

---

## Stage 1: Give

Offer something genuinely useful. One observation. One cue. One helpful piece of info.

**Technique corrections:**
> "Hey, one quick cue on the RDL if you want it, feel free to say no. Try pushing your hips back rather than down. Makes a big difference for the hamstring pull."

> "That overhead press is looking solid. One thing that helped my clients a lot at that stage is keeping the ribcage down. Want me to show you?"

**Logistics:**
> "The squat rack upstairs is usually free until 7 if you're finding it busy down here."

> "Just so you know, that machine has a broken pin. The one beside it is fine though."

**Simple acknowledgement (no advice needed):**
> "You've been at this consistently. That's the hard bit sorted."

> "How long have you been training? A few months? It's showing."

**Rule:** Say ONE thing. Don't give a lecture. Leave them wanting more.

---

## Stage 2: Position

Only after 2-3 positive interactions with the same person.

Drop this naturally, not as a pitch:

> "I've been building an app for the members here. It's completely free. Has all the programmes I give my PT clients. Might be useful if you're following a structured plan."

> "Do you use any apps for training? I built one for the gym here, it's free. Members seem to be finding it handy."

**Rule:** You're sharing a tool, not selling a service. Tone matters more than words here.

---

## Stage 3: Suggest

Only when they show interest. Even mild interest ("oh really?" or "what kind of app?") is enough.

> "Scan this and it'll take you straight to a free 3-Day Programme. It's what I give everyone who wants a proper starting point. Takes about 20-30 minutes a day."

> "Here, scan this. There's a free 3-day programme in there. It starts with a quick Capability Score so you can see exactly where you're at physically. Most people find it pretty eye-opening."

Hand them your phone showing the QR code, or point to the printed QR on your shirt/card.

## Conversation Threads That Work

These are follow-up questions that open real conversations without feeling like you're selling:

- "What are you working towards at the moment?"
- "How long have you been training here?"
- "Have you done any structured programme before, or mostly just coming in and doing your own thing?"
- "Are you finding you're making progress or does it feel like you're stuck a bit?"

The last two are gold. "Stuck" is the trigger word for coaching conversations.

---

## When Someone Says "I'm Fine on My Own"

Don't push. Respect it completely.

> "Totally get it. Offer stands if anything changes. Good luck with the training."

This response builds more trust than any pitch would. They'll remember it.

---

## Signs Someone is Ready for the Conversation

- They ask YOU a question about training
- They mention a goal or a problem unprompted
- They've been coming for 4+ weeks consistently
- They congratulate themselves on a lift or milestone
- They mention struggling ("my back's been killing me", "I just can't seem to shift the weight")

Any of these is a green light to move from Give to Position.

## Daily Tracking (Optional but Recommended)

Keep a note on your phone. After each shift:
- How many new people did I give something to?
- How many did I position the app to?
- How many QR codes did I share?

Target: 3 gives per shift. 1 position per shift. 1 QR per week per new person.
